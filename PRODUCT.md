## Register

brand

## Users
- **Mae Santos (Merchant)**: Fictional representative of Philippine MSME owners who sell products online (Instagram, social media, small websites). She is non-technical, budget-conscious, and wants to accept GCash, Maya, and card payments directly without paying high middleman commissions. She uses AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude) to help her run her business.
- **Alex Perez (Developer)**: Representative of freelance developers and agency owners building sites for local clients. He wants fast integrations, robust APIs, direct PayMongo integrations, and AI-native (MCP) configuration.

## Product Purpose
WizPay is a sovereign, unified checkout engine and storefront orchestration framework designed specifically for Philippine MSMEs. It allows merchants to accept payments directly into their own accounts via PayMongo, keeping 100% of their earnings with zero platform markups.

## Brand Personality
- **Friendly**: Approachable and warm, reassuring non-technical merchants that they can control their payments.
- **Accessible**: Easy to understand, simple steps, no gatekeeping, and translation ready for AI tools.
- **Direct**: Transparent value proposition, clean pricing explanation, zero platform jargon, and clear visual demonstrations.

## Anti-references
- **SaaS / AI Slop Clichés**: Saturated beige/cream backgrounds, text-gradient headings, tiny all-caps wide-tracked eyebrows above every section, and side-stripe cards.
- **Scammy Fintech Hype**: Over-promising financial success, high-pressure marketing slogans, and complex payment ecosystem jargon designed to confuse merchants.

## Design Principles
1. **Plain-Spoken Utility**: Explain the product in concrete nouns and verbs (e.g., "accept GCash payments" instead of "empower digital transitions").
2. **AI-First Integration**: Provide clean copy-paste prompts and developer tools (like MCP) that bridge the gap between AI code generation and visual setup.
3. **Frictionless Visual Proof**: Use interactive mockups that let users customize and test the payment flow live without reading code first.
4. **Legibility and Trust**: Keep typography clean, high-contrast, and structurally logical to reassure small merchants of security.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Contrast ratios must meet WCAG AA standards (>= 4.5:1 for body copy).
- Layouts must be responsive and testable down to small mobile screens.
- Avoid using complex or unfamiliar terms; write in local, natural English or easily translatable language.
